Fractions

Adding and Subtracting

When Adding and Subtracting fractions they have to have the same denominator

$$\frac{5}{10}-\frac{2}{10}=\frac{3}{10}$$
$$\frac{5}{10}+\frac{2}{10}=\frac{7}{10}$$

But when they don't have the same denominators
you multiply the bottom denominators and multiply
the numerator of the first fraction with the denominator of the second
then add it with the vice versa

$$\frac{a}{b}+\frac{c}{d}=\frac{a*d+b*c}{bd}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{4}=\frac{6}{8}=\frac{3}{4}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{4}=\frac{2}{8}=\frac{1}{4}$$

Multiplying

Multiplying is much easier since you just need to
multiple the numerators and denominators and it does not matter
if they have different numbers

$$\frac{4}{2}*\frac{1}{4}=\frac{4}{8}=\frac{1}{2}$$

Dividing

For Dividing you have to flip the second fraction and then multiply
the first fraction withe the flipped one

$$\frac{2}{5}*\frac{3}{2}=\frac{2*2}{5*3}=\frac{4}{15}$$

Questions

$$\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2}$$
$$\frac{2}{2}=1$$
