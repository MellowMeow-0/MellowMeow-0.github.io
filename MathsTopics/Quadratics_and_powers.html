Quadratics and powers

Multiplying and Dividing

When Multiplying and Dividing powers they actually get added and subtracted

$$a^2 * a^3=a^{2+3}=a^5$$
$$\frac {2^{4}} {2^{1}}=2^{4-1}=2^{3}=8$$

Brackets

when in numbers are in bracket they have to be worked on first
before doing the power outside

$$(2*4)^{2}=8^{2}=64$$

when a fraction is in a bracket the numerator and denomenator
be multiplied by the power outside the bracket

$$\left( \frac {2} {3} \right)^{2}=\frac {2^{2}} {3^{2}}=\frac {4} {9}$$

Negative Powers

The general rule to work out negative fractions is:

$$a^{-m}=\frac{1}{a^m}$$

This is to simplify the problem to make it much easier to answer
since it becomes converted into a normal power

$$3^{-2}=\frac{1}{3^2}=\frac{1}{9}$$

This can also be applied to fractions

$$\left( \frac {1} {3} \right)^{-1}= \frac {1} {\left( \frac {1} {3} \right)^{1}}=\frac {1} {\frac {1} {3}}=\frac {3} {1}=3$$
